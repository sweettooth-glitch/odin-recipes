Minestrone

Description

A warm and comforting dish that tastes even better the next day. Preperation may seem extensive and ingredients could go on forever, but its only because a good minestrone soup is endlessly variable. Use some of the ingredients or all of them, the key here is to include the essential flavour profiles and to give it time.

An addition not included on the ingredient list but which most definately wouldnt hurt? Anchovies. Trust me. Just chop finely and add them at at saut√® stage - they wont dominate the soup but rather, add a delicious umami finish. Serve with warm bread or garlic croutons

Ingredients

  • White onions
  • celery
  • carrots
  • garlic
  • smoked bacon or pancetta
  • tinned tomatoes
  • tomato paste
  • chicken stock
  • fresh parsely
  • red kindney beans
  • kale
  • dried pasta
  • salt, pepper, sugar, olive oil

    • Steps

    1. Finely dice all the vegetables and chop the bacon/pancetta into desired chunks
    2. Using a deep pan, heat the olive oil through and add the diced vegetables, gently sautee the vegetables until beginning to brown
    3. Add the bacon/pancetta and continue to sautee for 5 mins
    4. turn up the heat to medium/high and add tinned tomato and paste - you want to hear a sizzle as the tomato hits the pan. Continue stirring, cooking the tomato and vegetables down to a thick paste-15mins
    5. Add a generous pinch of salt, pepper and a heaped teasponn sugar
    6. Make up your stock according to package instructions and add to the pan, reduce heat to a gentle simmer and continue to cook for 1hr minimum
    7. Finely dice fresh parsely. Add kidney beans, pasta and torn kale to the stock - allow to cook until pasta is ready.
    8. Before service, sprinkle with fresh parsley and a generous grating of parmesan. Serve with your carbohydrate of choice.
